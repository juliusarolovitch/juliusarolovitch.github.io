About Me

Hello! I'm Julius, a junior at Carnegie Mellon studying ECE and Robotics. I'm fortunate to conduct research at CMU's Robotics Institute under Professors Maxim Likhachev and Howie Choset.

My current work focuses on applying generative learning to motion planning (SBPL), compressing graphs for accelerated planning under uncertainty (SBPL), and spatial optimization for heterogenous multi-agent Ergodic search (Biorobotics).

I am passionate about improving systems' ability to plan under uncertainty and leveraging learning to make more informed decisions. More broadly, I aim to build intelligent systems that help people.

Previously, I spent the summer of 2023 at Tel Aviv University's Robotics Lab working on learning for object recognition with underactuated hands, and the summer of 2024 at Johnson & Johnson doing systems integration for robotic manipulators on Ottava.

I enjoy traveling and learning languages, speaking English, Russian, Dutch, and French. Originally from Israel, I'm actively involved in Jewish life on CMU's campus, serving as President of CMU's Hillel. I also enjoy playing classical piano.
